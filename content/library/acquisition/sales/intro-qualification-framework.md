## What This Document Is
This blueprint defines how Waiz Media qualifies prospects on the Intro Call. It covers the FUN framework, how to apply each criteria, how to identify what actually motivates a prospect to buy, and how to set up the Demo Call so they show up ready to engage.

### By the end of this document, you will understand:
## The three non-negotiable qualification objectives
## How to apply FUN criteria — softly and only hard-qualifying when necessary
## How to identify and capture buying motivators
### How to frame the Demo Call so prospects show up instead of no-showing
### 📌  The North Star
The Intro Call has one output: a qualified prospect who is genuinely motivated to show up to the Demo.

You are not selling your solution. You are not handling objections. You are qualifying and creating desire for the next conversation.

### Objective 1 — Qualify the Prospect (FUN)
FUN is the qualification framework. A prospect must pass all three criteria before a Demo slot is offered. If they fail any one of them, do not book the Demo.

## F — Financially Qualified
Can they actually invest in the solution? This is about confirming budget capacity — not having a pricing conversation.

### Soft Qualification (Always Try This First)
Read conversational cues. These are the signals that a prospect may not be financially ready:

### Red flag: Keeps asking "How much does it cost?" before understanding the value
### Red flag: Asks "Do I have to put money up front?"
Red flag: Mentions cash flow problems (e.g., "I haven't closed a deal in 3 months")

If you're picking up soft cues clearly — move on. You don't need to hard qualify.

### Hard Qualification (Only When Soft Cues Fail)
Use only when you genuinely cannot read their financial position from the conversation.

### ⚠️  Warning — Hard Qualifying Lowers Show Rates
The more often you hard qualify, the more you introduce resistance and friction.

Use it as a last resort — not a default step.

**When they demand a number on the intro** — use the [Intro Call Script ballpark flow](/library/intro-call-script#if-they-push-price--what-does-it-cost): **wide** approved **[LOW]–[HIGH]** from the live pricing sheet, explain that fit depends on how fast they want to move and how large the build is, then ask if that ballpark works. One pass, then move on.

Script (return context — optional after ballpark): "Our average client makes an additional $[X] in their first 12 months working with us. They typically invest $[Y]. Does that range make sense for where you're at?"

Do **not** use a tight range (e.g. "$900 to $2,100") — they anchor low. The intro ballpark is intentionally **wide** for fit-check only, not a quote.

Give the answer and move on — do not explain ROI in detail, do not go down a rabbit hole.

### 🚨  What NOT to Do
Never quote exact package price, line items, or guarantees on the intro.

Never over-explain cost-per-acquisition or ROI math on this call.

Use only founder-approved **[LOW]** / **[HIGH]** from the pricing sheet — do not improvise.

## U — Urgency
Are they looking to solve this in the next couple of weeks? If they're not ready to move, they're not ready for a Demo.

## Urgency Signal
## What to Do
## Ready to solve this within weeks
## Proceed — book the Demo
## Vague or non-committal on timeline
Probe: ask why (timeline and blockers on intro — see [Intro Call Script — Stage 3](/library/intro-call-script#6--timeline-and-blockers)).

## Clearly not ready to act soon
Do not book Demo. Book a 15-min check-in 4 weeks out. Send resources in the meantime.

### 💡  Pro Tip
Don't try to close someone who isn't ready to act. Pushing a non-urgent prospect into a Demo wastes a slot and kills your show rate.

Book the check-in, stay in front of them, and wait for urgency to develop.

## N — Need
Is their primary problem something Waiz Media actually solves? If there's a mismatch between what they need and what you do, do not force it.

Match: Their biggest problem is inconsistent pipeline, no qualified conversations, or referral dependency → that's what we solve.

Mismatch: Their biggest problem is "I need more sales reps" or "my closer isn't converting" → that's not a lead gen problem. Don't try to fit the square peg.

### 📌  Rule
If the fit isn't there, say so. Disqualifying a bad-fit prospect is a win — it protects your Demo slots for people you can actually help.

## Objective 2 — Identify What Motivates Them to Buy
Qualification tells you if they can buy. Motivator identification tells you why they will. These are not the same thing — and most setters miss the second one entirely.

Buying motivators are the specific emotional and personal drivers that make a prospect say yes. Your job on the Intro Call is to surface them, capture them, and hand them to the Demo closer.

## What to Listen For
Pay attention to recurring words and emotionally charged phrases. Common patterns:

## What They Say
## What It Signals
## How to Use It on the Demo
### "I need consistency"
### Wants predictable, reliable results
## Frame the entire pitch around consistency and system reliability
### "Lead quality is the problem"
### Cares about caliber, not just volume
### Emphasize pre-qualification, education-first ads, and pre-seasoned leads
### "I'm drowning in work"
## Wants relief — time and mental load back
### Lead with done-for-you, no manual oversight required
### "Daughter going to college"
## Personal financial motivator
Anchor the close to that specific outcome: 3 deals/month → does that get her there?

### "10 agencies and they all sucked"
## Trust is broken — skepticism is high
## Flag for closer: objection prevention around proof and credibility is essential
## How to Capture It
### Take notes during the call — write down exact words and phrases they use
### Note personal details (daughter, retirement timeline, specific financial goals)
### Note emotional emphasis — what do they say with the most energy?
### Log it in your notes template and pass it to the Demo closer before the call
### ⭐  Why This Matters
The Demo closer needs your notes to run a sharp, personalized pitch.

Generic demos close at a fraction of the rate of demos that open with: "Hey, you mentioned trying to get your daughter into college — what would 3 extra deals a month mean for that?"

Your notes are the intel. Don't skip this.

## Objective 3 — Sell the Next Call
You do not pitch the solution on the Intro Call. You pitch the Demo. The goal is to make the prospect genuinely want to show up — not just agree to a time slot.

## The Framing Script
### 💡  Use This Frame
"So here's what we'll do on the next call. We're going to dive deeper into your business — it's going to be an actual marketing consultation. We'll analyze where you're at, identify what levers you need to pull to scale, and talk about what that roadmap looks like. And if you want to work with us, great, we can talk about that. But if not, at the very least, you'll walk away with so much clarity that you can go implement it all on your own for free."

## What This Frame Does
Positions the Demo as a consultation — not a sales pitch. Lowers resistance immediately.

Creates value regardless of purchase — they'll walk away with clarity either way. Removes the risk of showing up.

Dramatically increases show rates — prospects who expect value are far more likely to attend.

Primes them for discovery questions — they expect you to "dive deeper," so they show up ready to share.

### ⚠️  What Happens If You Skip This
If you don't frame the next call, prospects arrive defensive. They think it's a sales pitch and they guard themselves.

The frame flips the dynamic. They show up curious and open instead of closed and skeptical.

## What NOT to Do on the Intro Call
These are the five most common mistakes. Each one either kills show rates or wastes a Demo slot.

## Mistake
## Why It Kills You
## Trying to close or pitch the solution
You're not there yet. You're selling the Demo, not the service. Pitching here burns trust and skips the process.

### Going too deep on past attempts or objections
Don't run full discovery (Hell/Bridges/Heaven/Fuel) on the intro. Note what they tried; save reframes and proof for the Demo where they land hardest.

## Over-explaining ROI or pricing
Rabbit holes stall the call and invite price anchoring. Have a default answer prepared. Deliver it and move on.

## Pitching on command when they ask "what do you do?"
Use [Flip the Frame — company description](/library/flip-the-frame): Framework 1 (reclaim frame) or Framework 2 (pain → front end → relevance question), then discovery quartet. Do not feature-dump or qualify yourself to them.

## Not framing the next call properly
Without the consultation frame, prospects show up defensive. The frame is what creates an open, engaged Demo conversation.

## Not taking notes on motivators
The Demo closer needs your intel. If you didn't capture what drives them, the closer is walking in blind.

## The Right Mindset for the Intro Call
Prospects — especially reverse mortgage LOs who have been burned by agencies — are highly attuned to extractive sales energy. If you show up trying to get something from them, they'll resist. If you show up trying to help them gain clarity, they'll open up.

## Wrong Mindset
## Right Mindset
### "I need to close this person"
### "I need to figure out if I can actually help them"
### "Let me handle every objection now"
### "I'll note this and let the Demo closer address it properly"
### "More questions = more control"
### "Listen more than you talk — what they say matters more than what I say"
### "I have to justify our price"
### "I'll give a confident number and move on — no rabbit holes"
### "Get them to the Demo no matter what"
### "Only book the Demo if they genuinely qualify"
### ⭐  Energy Over Words
Prospects don't respond to your script. They respond to your intention.

Show up grounded, genuinely curious, and focused on helping them gain clarity — not on extracting a booking. That energy is what earns the Demo.
