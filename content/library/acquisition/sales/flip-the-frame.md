# Flip the Frame — Company Description Framework

## Purpose

Give setters and closers a repeatable way to answer **“What do you do?”** / **“Just give me the rundown”** without pitching on command — so reverse mortgage LOs **qualify themselves** instead of evaluating and dismissing you.

## Scope

Acquisition calls with reverse mortgage LO prospects. **Setter intro:** Framework 1 by default; Framework 2 when they need a description. Programs and implementation detail stay on the demo; **wide investment ballpark** only when required — [Intro Call Script](/library/intro-call-script#setter-boundaries-pricing-programs-depth).

## Owner

Sales leadership maintains copy; setters execute per [Intro Call Script](/library/intro-call-script).

---

## The context (why they ask)

When an LO says *“just give me the rundown”* or *“what do you guys do exactly?”* — they’re **setting the box**. They want you to pitch on command so they can judge and dismiss you.

Your job: **never step into that box.** Flip the script so they qualify themselves.

They’re not looking for another “marketing agency.” They want:

| Motivation | What it sounds like on calls |
|------------|------------------------------|
| **Consistency** | Feast-or-famine months, dry spells |
| **Predictability** | Leads that don’t convert or don’t show |
| **Freedom** | Tired of being a glorified call center chasing cold leads |
| **Production** | 4, 5, 10 fundings a month — not 1–2 |

Framework 2 speaks to these before you describe Waiz. Your **questions** do the selling — especially after they say *“yeah, that sounds familiar.”*

---

## Core principle

> We never qualify **to** the prospect. We want the prospect to qualify **to** us. They’re less receptive when we tell them — they convince themselves when they say it out loud. Nobody likes being told what to do.

The LO who says *“I only get a few leads a week and I know I need more conversations”* has told you why they need you — without you pitching.

---

## Framework 1 — Reclaim the frame (don’t pitch on command)

**When:** *“Just give me the quick rundown”* / *“What do you guys do exactly?”* / any demand for an on-the-spot pitch.

**Script:**

> Yeah, totally — happy to walk you through everything. But before I do, because what we do looks really different depending on where someone’s at in their business… do you mind if I ask you a couple quick questions first? Just to see if it even makes sense for your situation — because it’s not for everyone.

**Why it works:**

- You agreed and stayed warm — not defensive
- You took the frame back; **you** ask the questions now
- They’re qualifying themselves to you, not the other way around

**Next:** [Transition into discovery](#transition-into-discovery) → [Intro Call Script — Stage 3](/library/intro-call-script#stage-3--qualifying-questions).

---

## Framework 2 — Answer it, but speak to their pain first

**When:** They need a real description before they’ll engage — but you still refuse to feature-dump.

**Formula:** `Their ICP pain → What Waiz does for that ICP → Relevance question`

**Do not use one generic script.** Pick a track from [Intro ICP Tracks](/library/intro-icp-tracks):

| Track | Use when |
|-------|----------|
| **1 — Referral LO** | Referrals / sphere are the main pipeline |
| **2 — Marketing / system LO** | Already has ads, agency, leads, or a system — inconsistent or burned |
| **3 — Forward → reverse** | Forward-focused or new to reverse |

Live copy: [Intro Call Script — ICP tracks](/library/intro-call-script#icp-tracks-pick-one-before-who-we-are--demo-pitch).

**If they say yes / “that’s me”:** → [Transition into discovery](#transition-into-discovery).

**If they want how it works:** → [Intro — programs defer](/library/intro-call-script#if-they-push-how-does-it-work-programs--implementation). **If they want price:** → [Intro — ballpark range](/library/intro-call-script#if-they-push-price--what-does-it-cost).

## Transition into discovery

Use after Framework 1 **or** after they engage on Framework 2.

**Bridge:**

> Awesome. So before I go any deeper — I want to make sure I actually understand your situation, because the way we set things up is pretty tailored. Mind if I ask you a few things?

**Discovery quartet** (conversational — let them talk; capture verbatim):

1. > How are you generating leads right now?
2. > How many conversations are you having with homeowners per week, on average?
3. > What does production look like right now — and where do you want it to be?
4. > What’s been the biggest thing holding that back?

📋 **Listen more than you talk.** They’re convincing themselves; you’re holding the frame.

**Then complete operational qualifiers** on the intro (do not skip): licensed states, role, forward/reverse/both, timeline — [Intro Call Script — Stage 3](/library/intro-call-script#stage-3--qualifying-questions).

## Quick reference cheat sheet

| Situation | Move |
|-----------|------|
| “Give me the rundown” / “What do you do?” | **Framework 1** — questions first |
| They won’t move forward without a description | **Framework 2** — pain → front end → relevance question |
| They say “yeah, that’s me” | Transition bridge → discovery quartet → full Stage 3 |
| After any description | Always a **question** — never end on a monologue |
| “How does it work?” / programs | Defer to strategy call — [intro](/library/intro-call-script#if-they-push-how-does-it-work-programs--implementation) |
| “What does it cost?” | Wide **[LOW]–[HIGH]** + custom fit check — [intro](/library/intro-call-script#if-they-push-price--what-does-it-cost) |
| You feel defensive | Framework 1 — you don’t owe them a pitch |

## Script factory checklist (when adapting)

- [ ] Pain = **reverse LO front end** (consistency, shared leads, chasing) — not generic “make money online”
- [ ] Solution = **front end takeover** — ads through appointments — not “we sell leads”
- [ ] Ends with a **question** that invites self-qualification
- [ ] No exact quotes, line items, CPL, guarantees, or ROI math (wide ballpark only per intro script)
- [ ] Aligns with [Identity Core](../../../company/doctrine-identity-core-april-26.md)
