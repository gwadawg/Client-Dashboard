## Time Thieves
Every minute you spend with an unqualified prospect is a minute stolen from a qualified one.

I used to spend 45 minutes on calls with prospects who had $500 to their name who NEVER ended up closing because I was trying to pitch an unqualified prospect on a solution that no matter how much they liked, they wouldn’t be able to buy anyway.

If you want to make serious money in this business, you cannot afford to be a charity. Your time is your most valuable asset, and unqualified prospects are “time thieves”

However do not mistake a tough prospect with an unqualified one...

### Tough Prospect:
## Has money but is skeptical due to past experiences
## Asks hard questions but engages in the process
## Shows genuine interest but needs convincing
### May say "I need to think about it" but provides logical reasons
### Unqualified Prospect:
## Genuinely lacks financial resources
## Gives signals of financial distress
## Wants results without investment
Someone saying "I only want to pay when I get paid" might not be broke - they might just be protecting themselves after getting burned. Never assume broke until you've properly qualified.

You need to train your sales team to pick up on these “signals” that give indication they’re dealing with a broke lead, by building a green & red flag framework for them to know exactly who you CAN and CAN’T serve and to DQ the time thieves as early on as possible.

## Green Flags vs Red Flags
### To give you an example inside of our agency, a qualified prospect must have:
## Qualified Green Flags
### Business Structure Requirements:
### Brick and mortar location (not home-based or only virtual)
### Only anomaly is if they are home-based and do more than $10k+/mo
### Full medical spa with multiple treatment options (more than 3+ treatments)
### High-end devices (not just basic equipment)
Need to have treatments with an upsell potential of $1000+ or more on a treatment plan

### Financial Requirements:
$10,000+ monthly revenue OR acknowledged our price range and confirmed affordability

## Growth mindset - willing to invest for results
## Disqualification Red Flags
### Immediate Disqualifiers:
## Telemedicine only - no physical location
### Single treatment focus - only Botox, weight loss, or laser hair removal
We want to avoid high consumable commoditized treatments that are hard to position as different/unique, or that have minimal margin to work with

### Out-of-scope requests - testosterone therapy, general medical
Wrong business type - chiropractors, gyms, yoga studios (unless they offer aesthetics)

### Signals They’re Unqualified:
## Financial
### Desperation signals: "I just sold my car to fund this business"
### Debt implications: "I'm drowning in debt and need this to work"
### Resource limitations: "I only have one cheap device and it cost $3,000"
### Survival mode: "If this doesn't work, I'll have to close"
### Behavioral Red Flags:
## Unrealistic expectations - wants guaranteed results with no risk
### Blame mentality - everything is someone else's fault
These are all signals that could indicate that the lead is unqualified, but this doesn’t mean we just hang up the phone the minute we get a sense they’re broke - we need to throw a hard financial qualifier their way as soon as possible to prevent wasting sales time on a broke prospect.

## The Financial Qualification Process
### There are two types of disqualification signals:
### ICP Signals - they're not in your ideal customer profile
### Financial Signals - they can't afford your services
ICP disqualification is straightforward - you probe to see if they have anything you can help with. If not, you're honest and let them know they're not a good fit.

Financial disqualification requires finesse - you need to determine if they're genuinely broke or just protecting themselves.

## The Soft Financial Qualifier
When to Use: When you get subtle financial signals but they haven't explicitly said they're broke.

### The Script:
"John, let me stop you here quickly. I just want to make sure that what we do here actually makes sense for you - to make money. You gotta spend money, right? You put a dollar in, you get five dollars out - that's usually the art of advertising and how it works, and that's what you'd want to come to us for.

So I want to make sure that you obviously have that investment capital set aside or you have access to it in one form or another (whether it be through financing, credit, or whatnot) to invest in the growth of your own business.

With that being said, how much have you got set aside in savings, credit, that you're able to finance to put into the growth of the business (obviously if the ROI makes sense)?"

Key here when financially qualifying is not to go in too hard, frame it in their best interest to “save them time” and tie it back to if the ROI made sense

People will say they don’t have the money, but if I told someone with $0 they can give me $1000 and I’ll give them $5000 back each time they’ll find a way to get the money

### It all boils down to the perceived value of said thing they’re buying…
## The Hard Price Drop
When to Use: When they've indicated they have no money or given strong financial red flags after asking the above question and not getting a direct answer.

### The Script:
"The reason I ask, [NAME], is just to be transparent. Our program is expensive and requires an out-of-pocket investment, so I want to make sure we're making the best use of your time and ours. With that being said, is $3k-$5k a month in monthly marketing an investment that you'd be able to handle if the ROI made sense, of course?"

## The Financing Bridge
### When They Say They Don't Have Money:
"I understand. Let me ask you this - would you like me to show you how you can get funding so you can get your medspa to $100k/month like our other clients? Because that's exactly what we help our clients do, and many of them started in similar situations."

### Then Present Options:
## Business credit lines
## Equipment financing
## SBA loans
## Revenue-based financing
### Payment plans (if available)
ALWAYS tie funding to their desired outcome, not just to paying your fee. They're not getting financing to pay you - they're getting financing to achieve their goals.

Results-Based Thinking Script: "So the question becomes: Do you want to stay where you are, or do you want to get to $100k/month? Because if you want to get there, you'll need to invest in the systems that get you there. The funding is just the vehicle."

## Graceful Disqualification Strategies
## ICP Disqualification Script
"John, I really appreciate you taking the time to speak with me today. Based on what you've shared, I want to be completely transparent with you - we specifically work with full-service medical spas that offer body sculpting and facial aesthetics, and it sounds like you're focused primarily on weight loss.

I don't want to waste your time or mine by trying to force a fit that isn't there. You deserve to work with someone who specializes in your exact situation.

Let me ask you this - do you have any plans to add aesthetic treatments like CoolSculpting, Morpheus8, or facial fillers in the next 6-12 months? Because if you do, I'd love to reconnect at that time."

## Financial Disqualification Script
"Sarah, I really appreciate your honesty about your current financial situation. I've been in business long enough to know that timing is everything, and it sounds like right now might not be the perfect time for you to make this kind of investment.

I respect that, and I don't want to put you in a position where you're stretching beyond what's comfortable.

Here's what I'd like to do: I'm going to send you some resources that can help you start improving your patient flow right away at no cost. And when you're in a position where you've got some capital set aside for growth, I'd love to reconnect and see if we can help you scale to that next level.

### Does that sound fair?"
### Follow-up Actions:
## Send valuable free resources
## Add to nurture sequence
## Set calendar reminder for 6-month follow-up
## Note their situation in CRM for future reference
## The Referral Opportunity
Before ending the call: "One last thing - do you know any other medical spa owners who might be in a better position to invest in growth right now? I'd be happy to help them the same way we've helped our other clients, and I always take care of people who send good referrals our way."

## The Re-Qualification Process
Sometimes prospects change during the conversation and signals pop up the further you go into the call, this is where we have to re-qualify the prospect:

### Mid-Call Red Flags:
## New financial concerns emerge
## Business situation is worse than indicated
## Decision-making authority is unclear
## Unrealistic expectations surface
Usually comes with the ego-driven prospects who give you glorified numbers in the early stages, then after breaking down the numbers with them you realize it doesn’t make sense, and then they tell you the ACTUAL numbers…

### The Re-Qualification Script:
"John, let me pause here for a second. Based on what you just shared, I want to make sure I'm understanding your situation correctly. It sounds like [summarize new information]. Is that accurate?

Given that information, I want to be completely honest with you about whether what we do makes sense for your situation right now..." (Then go into the financial qualification flow…)

### Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases
Below I’ve broken down some unique, but not uncommon scenarios that may arise with “special snowflake” leads and how to handle them:

## The Wealthy Wrong-ICP Prospect
Scenario: Chiropractor with $2M annual revenue wants marketing help.

### Decision Framework:
### Can we help them? Do they offer any aesthetic treatments?
### Will they expand? Are they open to adding aesthetic services?
### Referral potential? Do they know medical spa owners?
### Opportunity cost? Is this time better spent with qualified prospects?
### The Script:
"Dr. Johnson, I can see you've built an incredibly successful practice, and I really respect what you've accomplished. Our specialty is specifically medical spas and aesthetic treatments, which isn't your focus.

However, I'm curious - have you ever considered adding aesthetic services like Botox or dermal fillers to your practice? Many chiropractors are finding it's a great way to diversify revenue and serve their existing patients better.

If that's something you might explore, I'd love to continue this conversation. If not, I'd be happy to refer you to someone who specializes in chiropractic marketing."

## The Broke ICP Prospect
Scenario: Perfect medical spa fit but genuinely lacks financial resources.

### The Long-Term Play:
### Provide immediate value - free resources, advice, connections
## Stay connected - regular check-ins and market updates
### Help them grow - refer them to funding sources, business coaches
## Be patient - many successful clients started broke
This is a perfect opportunity to consider downselling these people on a DIY / DWY product to get the ball rolling & make them some money so that they can then invest into your DFY product

### The Script:
"Sarah, I can see you have exactly the type of practice we love working with, and I believe you're going to be incredibly successful. The challenge is timing - you need some runway before you can invest in growth marketing.

Here's what I'd like to do: I'm going to send you our complete patient acquisition blueprint that you can implement yourself. I'll also connect you with a business coach who specializes in medical spa funding.

When you're ready to scale - and I believe you will be - I want to be the first person you call. Does that sound fair?"

## The Borderline Prospect
Scenario: Meets most criteria but one significant concern.

### The Evaluation Process:
### Identify the specific concern - financial, ICP, urgency, or authority
### Determine if it's fixable - can the concern be addressed?
### Assess probability - what are the odds they'll close?
### Consider opportunity cost - what else could you be doing with this time?
### Decision Matrix:
### High probability + fixable concern = Continue
### Low probability + major concern = Disqualify
### Medium probability + minor concern = Continue with adjusted approach
### High value + any concern = Continue (worth the risk)
## Key Takeaways
Time is your most valuable asset - protect it by qualifying ruthlessly but professionally

Tough prospects aren't unqualified prospects - learn to distinguish between skepticism and inability

## Financial qualification requires finesse - use soft qualifiers before hard price drops
Graceful disqualification preserves relationships - today's unqualified prospect might be tomorrow's referral source, and lean on referrals to get them over the fence

Systems and documentation are critical - qualification decisions should be trackable and improvable

Team training prevents inconsistency - everyone should qualify using the same standards, make an internal SOP on green & red flags so the team is fully aligned on who they can & can’t sell.
