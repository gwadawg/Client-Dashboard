# Intro Call Script

## Purpose

Word-for-word guide for setter intro calls: low-pressure discovery, operational qualification, demo booking, and pre-call nurture — without pitching programs or pricing on this call.

## Scope

Stage 1 intro calls (all entry types). Qualification rules (FUN, Boot Camp, financial hard-qualify) live in [Intro Call Qualification Framework](/library/intro-qualification-framework). Do not use Hell/Bridges/Heaven/Fuel on this call — that depth is for demo/closer discovery.

## Owner

See [domain owners](../../_inventory/domain-owners.md): **setter**.

## When To Use

Every live intro: booked slot, early confirmation call, dialer connect, or intro no-show recovery. Pick the opening in [Stage 1 — Introduction](#stage-1--introduction-pick-one-opening), then run [Stages 2–7 (shared)](#stages-27-shared).

## Quality Bar

- Waiz voice: qualified conversations, acquisition system, strategic partner ([Identity Core](../../company/doctrine-identity-core-april-26.md)).
- Conversation, not pitch — prospect should feel evaluated for fit, not sold.
- Programs and implementation depth → demo with closer. **Ballpark investment range** only when they won’t move without a number — wide range + custom framing per [pricing boundaries](#setter-boundaries-pricing-programs-depth) and approved [pricing sheet](../../company/overview-money-model-april-26.md#pricing-rule).
- Not DFY-qualified → Boot Camp, not demo ([Disqualifying and Financial Qualification](/library/financial-qualification)).

## Metrics

- North star: **qualified prospect who shows up to the demo** (see [KPIs](../../kpis/README.md)).
- Track intro → demo book rate and demo show rate per setter EOD.

---

## North star

**What this call is:** A short discovery conversation — not a sales call, not a demo. You gather information about their situation, see if they’re a fit, and if they qualify, you book them with the right person to walk through how everything works in detail.

**What this call is not:** Closing, full objection handling, or explaining programs, packages, and investment. Save that for the demo.

**Your job:** Make it feel like a natural conversation. They should feel like **we’re deciding if they qualify for the next step** — not that they’re being pitched. That frame increases show rates and how much they value the demo.

**Handoff target:** After a qualified intro, the closer gets FUN confirmation, motivator notes, a booked demo, pre-call video commitment, and verified contact info.

---

## Call checklist (5 things)

Run the call in this order. **Don’t hang up until all five are checked.** Word-for-word script for each step is below.

- [ ] **1. Frame** — [Stage 2](#stage-2--expectations-while-building-trust): not a sales call; your job is fit + no wasted time; quick prep for strategy call; can send resources
- [ ] **2. Qualifying questions** — [Stage 3](#stage-3--qualifying-questions): discovery + states, role, forward/reverse, lead gen, production, timeline. FUN pass or route out ([framework](/library/intro-qualification-framework)) — don’t book if not qualified
- [ ] **3. Sold demo** — [Stage 4](#stage-4--show-fit--sell-the-demo): [ICP track](#icp-tracks-pick-one-before-who-we-are--demo-pitch) picked, label back, track-specific pitch, they agreed
- [ ] **4. Tie down appointment + confirm info** — [Stage 5](#stage-5--book-the-demo) book in GHL on the call + [Stage 7](#stage-7--contact-and-message-verification) date/time locked, phone/email correct, they get our messages, calendar accepted
- [ ] **5. Tie down — commit to watching material** — [Stage 6](#stage-6--pre-call-content-tie-down): pre-call video sent, they said they’ll watch before the strategy call

📋 After hang-up: [post-call notes](#post-call-notes-for-closer) for [CLOSER NAME].

## Setter boundaries (pricing, programs, depth)

Prospects get frustrated when the setter can’t answer programs and pricing — **because this call isn’t built for that.** Use these lines; do not improvise packages or numbers.

**Frame rule:** Never pitch on command. Full methodology: [Flip the Frame — company description](/library/flip-the-frame).

### If they ask “What do you do?” / want a rundown

Full framework: [Flip the Frame — company description](/library/flip-the-frame). **Do not pitch on command.**

**Default — Framework 1 (reclaim the frame):**

> Yeah, totally — happy to walk you through everything. But before I do, because what we do looks really different depending on where someone’s at in their business… do you mind if I ask you a couple quick questions first? Just to see if it even makes sense for your situation — because it’s not for everyone.

📋 → [Discovery transition](#discovery-transition-then-full-qualifiers). Do not feature-dump.

**If they need a description first — Framework 2 (pick ICP track):**

📋 [Pick track](#icp-tracks-pick-one-before-who-we-are--demo-pitch) from qualifying — use that track’s **“Who we are”** script (not the generic version).

📋 If **yes** / “that’s familiar” → [Discovery transition](#discovery-transition-then-full-qualifiers).

### If they push “how does it work?” (programs / implementation)

> Totally fair — and you deserve a real answer, not a generic one. How we’d set it up for *you* — programs, timeline, what we run vs what you run — is exactly what [CLOSER NAME] walks through on the strategy call using what we learned today. I’ll note your questions so he hits them first. For now I just need a few more minutes to see if it even makes sense to book that call.

📋 Do **not** explain packages, deliverables, or guarantee language on this call.

### If they push price / “what does it cost?”

**First try** — defer without stonewalling:

> I want you to get a real number, not something pulled out of thin air. Investment depends on how aggressive you want to grow and what we’d actually build for your states and production — [CLOSER NAME] nails that on the strategy call. If you need a ballpark now, I can give you a range — fair?

**If yes — ballpark range (wide, custom, then fit check):**

Use the **current approved intro range** from the live pricing sheet (founder-approved). Do not invent numbers.

> Got it. We have different programs — ballpark, you’re looking at somewhere around **[LOW]** on the lighter side, up to around **[HIGH]** if you want the full build and faster ramp. Everything’s custom to the LO, so where you land depends on how fast you want to move and how big of a rollout you want — volume, states, how much we’re running on the front end.
>
> The strategy call is where we map that to *your* situation and give you an actual recommendation — not a generic quote.
>
> With that wide range in mind — does something in that ballpark realistically work for you if the fit and ROI make sense?

📋 **Rules for the range:**

- **[LOW]** / **[HIGH]** = approved sheet only (update when pricing changes).
- Keep the range **wide** — purpose is ballpark fit, not a quote.
- Anchor **customization** (speed + size of build) — not “cheap option vs expensive option.”
- One pass, then **move on** — no ROI math, no line-item breakdown, no negotiating.
- If they fixate on **[LOW]** only → “Most LOs at your production level aren’t at the bottom of that range — [CLOSER NAME] will show you what actually fits.”

**If they still won’t move without exact pricing or custom deal terms** → tag Gabriel per [Watchshift SOP](/library/watchshift); note in CRM.

**If ballpark is clearly out of reach** → [Disqualifying and Financial Qualification](/library/financial-qualification) / Boot Camp — do not force a demo.

Then confirm **FUN — F** per [Intro Call Qualification Framework](/library/intro-qualification-framework).

### If they raise a deep objection

Capture it in notes. Do **not** reframe on this call. Point to demo or send a [pre-call video](../marketing/pre-call-objection-videos.md#quick-send-by-objection) if trust/credibility is the gap. See [Objection Handling Hub](/library/objection-handling-hub).

> Hey, [NAME]?
>
> Hey [NAME], this is [SETTER NAME] with Waiz Media — how’s it going?
>
> I know we had something on the calendar for right now. You were looking into growing your reverse mortgage business — still the right time to chat for a few minutes?

📋 Wait for confirmation. If wrong person or bad time → reschedule on **setter calendar** (BAMFAM — don’t “call you later” without a hold).

**If they ask “What do you guys do?”** → [Flip the Frame — Part 1 or 2](#if-they-ask-what-do-you-do--want-a-rundown), then Stage 2 or Stage 3.

Is that fair?

📋 If pushback (“why are you calling early?”): stay calm — frame as **their** benefit (fit check, no wasted time). Philosophy: [No Shows — Intro Call Reality](/library/show-rate-levers#the-intro-call-reality).

> Perfect — this’ll only be a few minutes.

→ Continue to [Stage 2](#stage-2--expectations-while-building-trust).

### Opening 3 — Dialer / impromptu

> Hey, [NAME]?
>
> Hey [NAME], this is [SETTER NAME] with Waiz Media — how’s it going?
>
> I’ll be quick — do you have two minutes? I’ve got a few questions to see what the best next step would be for you, if any.

**If they have time** → Stage 2.

**If they don’t have time:**

> No stress at all. Let’s get something on the calendar so we’re not playing phone tag.

📋 **BAMFAM** — book the intro on the **setter’s** GHL calendar for the soonest realistic slot. Confirm text/email while scheduling.

- Document exact GHL calendar, duration, and confirmation text when ops confirms (placeholder until then).

→ If they stay on the line, continue to [Stage 2](#stage-2--expectations-while-building-trust).

### Opening 4 — Intro no-show recovery

> Hey, [NAME]?
>
> Hey [NAME], it’s [SETTER NAME] from Waiz Media — we had you down for [TIME] today to talk about growing your reverse business. I tried you at the scheduled time and wanted to make sure you’re still interested.

**If they engage now:**

> No worries at all — happens. Do you have a few minutes now, or should we grab a better time?

📋 Full no-show sequence (wait, double dial, texts, front desk): [No Shows and Maximizing Show Rates](/library/show-rate-levers). If they’re on the phone, run Stages 2–7 like a normal intro.

**If reschedule only:** book intro or demo on setter calendar; confirm contact info (Stage 7).

→ Continue to [Stage 2](#stage-2--expectations-while-building-trust) when live.

## Stages 2–7 (shared)

### Stage 2 — Expectations while building trust

**Goal:** Pre-handle the objections before they come up. Keep this short — you do **not** need to list every question you’ll ask.

> Well, pleasure to meet you — appreciate you jumping on.
>
> Real quick so we’re on the same page:
>
> **One** — this isn’t a sales call and it’s not a demo. I’m not here to pitch you on anything.
>
> **Two** — my only job on this call is to make sure we’re not wasting your time and that we can actually help you. If it’s not a fit, I’ll tell you. If it is, we’ll get you booked with [CLOSER NAME] for the strategy call.
>
> **Three** — this’ll be quick. I just need enough from you so we have everything prepared for that strategy call — so when you talk to [CLOSER NAME], you’re not repeating yourself and he can go straight into what matters for *your* business.
>
> **Four** — if you want to learn more about us before that call, I can send you everything you need — short videos, our socials, whatever helps. You don’t have to guess who we are.
>
> Sound fair?

**Same frame, conversational (pick one delivery — don’t read both):**

> Quick heads-up before we dive in — this isn’t a sales call, I’m not pitching you. I’m just here to see if we can actually help you and not waste your time. It’s a short call so we’ve got everything lined up for your strategy call with [CLOSER NAME], and if you want to learn more about us before then, I can send you whatever you need — videos, links, all of that. is that cool with you?

**Permission to start:**

> Cool — just curious... what got you interested in looking into this in the first place?

📋 Listen. Note motivators in their exact words ([framework — motivators](/library/intro-qualification-framework#objective-2--identify-what-motivates-them-to-buy)).

**If they ask for resources now** (don’t wait until end of call):

> Yeah, I’ll send that over — I’ve got a couple short videos that answer most of the “who are you guys” stuff. I’ll text them after we hang up.

📋 Pick from [pre-call objection videos](../marketing/pre-call-objection-videos.md#quick-send-by-objection) when you know the gap (trust, burnt by agency, etc.). Stage 6 still ties down watch-before-demo.

---

### Stage 3 — Qualifying questions

Dig when answers are vague — don’t accept surface-level. **Listen more than you talk** after Framework 1 or 2; let them say out loud why they need help.

#### Discovery transition (then full qualifiers)

Use after [Framework 1 or 2](#if-they-ask-what-do-you-do--want-a-rundown), or after Stage 2 permission if they didn’t ask for a rundown yet.

> Awesome. So before I go any deeper — I want to make sure I actually understand your situation, because the way we set things up is pretty tailored. Mind if I ask you a few things?

**Discovery quartet** — ask naturally; capture verbatim:

1. > How are you generating leads right now?
2. > How many conversations are you having with homeowners per week, on average?
3. > What does production look like right now — and where do you want it to be?
4. > What’s been the biggest thing holding that back?

📋 When they answer #4, you often have the demo closer’s best material. Don’t interrupt — note exact words.

**Operational qualifiers** — complete every call (fill gaps if already covered above):

#### 1 — Licensed states

> Which states are you licensed in?

📋 Note all states. Flag if outside Waiz’s active footprint per ops guidance.

#### 2 — Role

> Are you at a brokerage, a lender, or do you own the shop?

#### 3 — Product focus (forward / reverse / both)

> Are you doing forward mortgages, reverse, or both right now?

**If forward-only but exploring reverse:**

> Got it — what has you looking at reverse versus finding a partner that could help you grow forward volume instead?

📋 You need a real reason. Weak or “just curious” → note for closer; may affect fit.

#### 4 — Lead generation today

> How are you currently generating leads?

📋 Skip if covered in discovery quartet #1 — otherwise ask. Follow-ups: referrals only? Paid? Purchased leads? Mix?

#### 5 — Production

> What does production look like on average — deals per month?

📋 Skip or shorten if covered in discovery quartet #3 — then confirm goal numbers for the closer.

**If monthly is inconsistent:**

> Makes sense — what did the full **last year** look like? And what does a good month vs a slow month look like for you?

#### 6 — Timeline and blockers

> Assuming everything checks out on our side, how soon are you looking to get something in place?

> What would hold you back from making a decision if the fit is there?

📋 Capture timeline + real blockers (partner, cash flow, other deals, etc.).

---

#### After qualifying — routing

**FUN gate** — Before Stage 4, confirm per [Intro Call Qualification Framework](/library/intro-qualification-framework):

- **F** — Can they invest? (soft cues first; hard qualify only if needed)
- **U** — Moving in the next few weeks?
- **N** — Problem we solve (pipeline, lead quality, consistency — not “hire more closers”)


| Outcome                         | What to do                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **FUN pass**                    | Continue to Stage 4                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Not urgent / clear blockers** | Do **not** force demo. Send [pre-call nurture](../marketing/pre-call-objection-videos.md); book **15-min check-in** closer to their decision window ([framework — Urgency](/library/intro-qualification-framework#u--urgency)) |
| **FUN fail / not DFY**          | [Disqualifying and Financial Qualification](/library/financial-qualification) — Boot Camp or polite disqualify                                                                                                        |

## ICP tracks (pick one before “who we are” + demo pitch)

Pick from qualifying (how they get leads + forward/reverse). Full reference: [Intro ICP Tracks](/library/intro-icp-tracks).

| Track | Pick when… |
|-------|------------|
| **1 — Referral LO** | Mostly referrals, sphere, past clients — production swings when that slows |
| **2 — Marketing / system LO** | Already runs ads, agency, bought leads, or a funnel — inconsistent or burned |
| **3 — Forward → reverse** | Forward-focused or new to reverse — building reverse as the growth path |

📋 Log track in CRM notes. If two fit, lead with **primary** lead source today.

---

### Track 1 — Referral LO

**Who we are** (if they asked before/during qualify):

> Yeah, so — a lot of reverse LOs are **great** at the job — clients love them, referrals come in when the market’s hot — but the pipeline is whoever thought of them that month. Some months you’re slammed, then it goes quiet. It’s not a skill problem; there’s just **nothing booked in** when referrals slow.
>
> We build a **second engine** — ads, pre-qual, appointments — so you’re not waiting on your sphere for a good month. Referrals can stay; we make sure you’ve got qualified conversations on the calendar.
>
> Does that sound like where you’re at?

**Demo pre-pitch** (after [label](#label--confirm-understanding) + [lean out](#lean-out)):

> You’re solid once you’re in front of someone — you just can’t control how many conversations show up. That’s exactly who we built this for.
>
> Strategy call with [CLOSER NAME] — he works with referral-heavy LOs who wanted **predictability** without ditching what already works. He’ll map appointments on top of referrals, what it takes to hit [THEIR GOAL], and what we run vs what you keep. Investment is his lane — you leave with a plan either way. Fair?

---

### Track 2 — Marketing / system LO

**Who we are:**

> Yeah, so — a lot of reverse LOs have **tried** marketing — Facebook, agency, bought leads, a funnel — but it’s junk, shared with other LOs, or you’re chasing people who don’t remember opting in. You’re **busy**, but the front end still isn’t **predictable**.
>
> We run the full front end **only for reverse** — ads, landing page, pre-qual, booking — per LO. Not a generic lead vendor; we fix the system, not sell volume that doesn’t fund.
>
> Does that sound like what you’ve been dealing with?

**Demo pre-pitch:**

> You’ve got pieces moving — it just hasn’t given you a **reliable** pipeline. We see that constantly.
>
> [CLOSER NAME] on a strategy call will look at what you run now — or what burned you — and what we’d **keep, change, or replace**. He’ll show what’s worked at your production level and what it takes to hit [THEIR GOAL]. Full implementation and investment are on that call if you want them. Fair?

📋 Consider [burnt](../marketing/pre-call-objection-videos.md#quick-send-by-objection) or [leads](../marketing/pre-call-objection-videos.md#quick-send-by-objection) pre-call video later.

---

### Track 3 — Forward → reverse

**Who we are:**

> A lot of LOs we talk to are **strong on forward** — they know how to work clients — but reverse is a different acquisition game. Different homeowner, different message — and if the front end’s wrong, you’re learning two hard things at once.
>
> We do **only reverse** — we run the front end so you’re on calls with people who want to talk reverse, while you focus on product and closing.
>
> Does that sound like what you’re going for?

**Demo pre-pitch:**

> You want a **clear path into reverse** without building the whole acquisition machine yourself. That’s our lane.
>
> [CLOSER NAME] will walk how LOs at your stage entered reverse, what breaks when people DIY the front end, and what we’d run for you. He’ll map [THEIR GOAL] and timeline — implementation detail on that call if you want it. Fair?

📋 Must have **why reverse?** from Stage 3 — note for closer.

---

### Stage 4 — Show fit + sell the demo

You are **not** pitching Waiz as the offer. You are pitching the **demo / strategy call**.

📋 Use the **demo pre-pitch** for the [ICP track](#icp-tracks-pick-one-before-who-we-are--demo-pitch) you picked — not the generic pitch below.

#### Label — confirm understanding

> Gotcha — so correct me if I’m off: you’re licensed in [STATES], [ROLE], focused on [FORWARD/REVERSE/BOTH], getting leads from [SOURCE], doing about [PRODUCTION], and you’re looking to move on this [TIMELINE] if the fit is right — and [BLOCKER/MOTIVATOR] is a big part of why. Did I get that right?

📋 Wait for yes or correction.

> Are you cool if I share what I’m thinking?

#### Lean out (all tracks)

> Would you say that sounds like you — you’re in a position to take the next step if the strategy makes sense?

#### Permission (all tracks)

> If you’re open to it, I’ll tell you what I’d recommend.

📋 Then deliver that track’s **demo pre-pitch** from [ICP tracks](#icp-tracks-pick-one-before-who-we-are--demo-pitch). Ends with *“Fair?”* / *“Does that sound fair?”*

**Generic fallback** (only if track is unclear — pick a track instead when possible):

> Strategy call with [CLOSER NAME] — he’ll use everything from today to map your ops, what’s worked for LOs like you, and what it takes to hit [THEIR GOAL]. Programs and investment are his lane. Fair?

**Temp check (all tracks):**

> Do you think that would actually help you?

📋 If hesitation → surface it; don’t book through resistance. Light clarify only — no full objection battle.

**Logistics:**

> It’s a Google Meet — be at your computer. Plan about 45 minutes; we’ll block an hour for questions.

---

### Stage 5 — Book the demo

📋 **Stop** — items **1–3** on the [call checklist](#call-checklist-5-things) must be done before you book. Items **4–5** before you hang up.

**Try live transfer first:**

> Are you near your computer? I can see if [CLOSER NAME] is free to jump on now.

**Same day:** > What time later today works?

**Next day:** > No worries — what about tomorrow?

**Two days out:** > Gotcha — what about the day after?

**Three+ days out:**

> He’s pretty packed — let me see what I can do. It usually doesn’t let me book that far out, but if there’s a time that **for sure** works for you, I’ll try to make it work.

**Tie-down:**

> And is there any reason [DATE AND TIME] wouldn’t work for you?

🔴 **Book in GHL while they’re on the line.** Do not verbally confirm without logging the appointment.

---

### Stage 6 — Pre-call content tie-down

> Cool — you’re in for [DATE AND TIME]. Calendar invite coming in a second.
>
> Based on what you mentioned — [UNIQUE ATTRIBUTE / OBJECTION] — I’ve got a short video that’ll help you get the most out of the strategy call. Want me to send it?

Pick video: [Quick send by objection](../marketing/pre-call-objection-videos.md#quick-send-by-objection) — send **prospect page** link, not YouTube.

> If I send that — you’ll watch it before the call, yeah?

> I’ll send that with the invite. Our socials are there if you want extra research, but that video is the main prep.

---

### Stage 7 — Contact and message verification

> Before we hop off — what’s the best number and email for you? I want to make sure what we have in the system is right.

> Have you been getting our texts and emails okay?

**If no / wrong contact:**

- Update GHL while on the call
- Resend test message or “say yes when you get this”
- Re-send calendar invite if needed

> Could you confirm the calendar invite on your end? Just want to make sure nothing double-books over it.

**Once confirmed:**

> You’re set. I’ll send these notes to [CLOSER NAME] — see you [DATE AND TIME].
