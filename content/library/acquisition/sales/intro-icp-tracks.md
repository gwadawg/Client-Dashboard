# Intro ICP Tracks — Referral, Marketing, Forward-to-Reverse

## Purpose

Three ICP-specific talk tracks for setter intros: **“who we are”** (Framework 2) and **demo pre-pitch** (Stage 4). Same call flow everywhere — pick the track that matches how they actually get business.

## Scope

Setter intro calls only. Framework 1 (reclaim frame) stays universal — [Flip the Frame](/library/flip-the-frame). Programs/pricing still on strategy call.

## Pick a track (routing)


| Track                                                           | Pick when…                                                                                                                 |
| --------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **[1 — Referral LO](#track-1--referral-lo)**                    | Lead gen is mostly **referrals, sphere, past clients, networking** — production swings when referrals slow                 |
| **[2 — Marketing / system LO](#track-2--marketing--system-lo)** | They already run **ads, an agency, bought leads, or a marketing system** — results are inconsistent or they’ve been burned |
| **[3 — Forward → reverse](#track-3--forward--reverse)**         | **Forward-focused** or **new to reverse** — exploring reverse as growth (use their answer to “why reverse?”)               |


**If two fit:** lead with the **primary** source of leads today. Note the second in CRM for the closer.

**Live script:** [Intro Call Script — ICP tracks](/library/intro-call-script#icp-tracks-pick-one-before-who-we-are--demo-pitch).

## Track 1 — Referral LO

### Who we are (Framework 2)

> Yeah, so — you know how there's a lot of reverse LOs out there who are really good at what they do, they know the product inside and out, they genuinely want to help their clients... but every month it's kind of a guessing game? Like, some months are great, and then out of nowhere it just dries up — and it's not because they're bad at sales, it's just because they're not having enough conversations with the right people.
>
> And a lot of times what happens is they end up buying leads that are shared with five other LOs, or they're spending their own time chasing people who don't qualify or even pick up the phone. So they're working hard, but the front end of their business is just inconsistent.
>
> What we do is we take over that entire front end — the  lead gen, prospecting, the pre-qualification, the appointment setting — so that the only thing you're doing is showing up to calls with people who actually want to talk about a reverse.
>
> Does that sound like something that would be relevant to where you're at right now?
>
> Does that sound like where you’re at?

### Demo pre-pitch (after label + lean out)

**Fit:**

> Based on what you said — you’re solid once you’re in front of someone, but you can’t control how many conversations show up — that’s exactly the LOs we built this for.

**Pitch:**

> I’d get you on a strategy call with [CLOSER NAME]. He works with a lot of referral-heavy LOs who wanted **predictability** without abandoning what already works.
>
> He’ll map what a steady flow of appointments would look like on top of your referrals, what it’d take to hit [THEIR GOAL], and what we’d actually run vs what you keep doing. Programs and investment are his lane — but you’ll leave with a clear plan either way.
>
> Does that sound fair?

## Track 2 — Marketing / system LO

### Who we are (Framework 2)

> Yeah, so — you know how a lot of reverse LOs have **tried** marketing already? Facebook, an agency, bought leads, maybe even a funnel — but it’s either junk, shared with a bunch of other LOs, or you’re chasing people who don’t remember opting in. So you’re **busy**, but the front end still doesn’t feel **predictable**.
>
> What we do is run the full front end **only for reverse** — ads, landing page, pre-qual, booking — customized per LO. We’re not another generic lead vendor; we’re trying to fix the system, not sell you more volume that doesn’t fund.
>
> Does that sound relevant to what you’ve been dealing with?

### Demo pre-pitch (after label + lean out)

**Fit:**

> You’ve already got pieces in motion — the issue is it hasn’t given you a **reliable** pipeline. We work with LOs in that exact spot all the time.

**Pitch:**

> Strategy call with [CLOSER NAME] is the right next step. He’ll look at what you’re running now — or what burned you before — and show what we’d **keep, change, or replace** so you’re not repeating the same mistake.
>
> He’ll walk through what’s worked for LOs at your production level and what it’d take to hit [THEIR GOAL] consistently. If you want the full implementation picture and investment, he covers that there — but you’ll at least leave knowing what to fix.
>
> Does that sound fair?

**Pre-call video hint:** Often [burnt by agency](../marketing/pre-call-objection-videos.md#quick-send-by-objection) or [leads](../marketing/pre-call-objection-videos.md#quick-send-by-objection).

## Track 3 — Forward → reverse

### Who we are (Framework 2)

> Yeah, so — a lot of LOs we talk to are **strong on forward** — they know how to work clients — but reverse is a different acquisition game. The product’s different, the homeowner’s different, and if the front end isn’t built right, you’re learning two hard things at once.
>
> What we do is **only reverse** — we run the whole front end so you’re showing up to calls with people who already want to talk about a reverse, while you focus on getting sharp on the product and closing.
>
> Does that sound like what you’re trying to do?

### Demo pre-pitch (after label + lean out)

**Fit:**

> You’re not looking for “more marketing tips” — you want a **clear path into reverse** without building an entire acquisition machine from scratch. That’s the lane we’re in.

**Pitch:**

> [CLOSER NAME] on a strategy call is where that gets real. He’ll walk through how LOs at your stage have entered reverse, what usually breaks when people try to DIY the front end, and what we’d run for you so you’re not guessing.
>
> He’ll map what it’d take to hit [THEIR GOAL] and, if you want, what full implementation looks like — but minimum you leave with a game plan for reverse that matches **your** states and timeline.
>
> Does that sound fair?

**Qualifying must-have:** “Why reverse vs growing forward volume?” — note answer for closer.
